# Protocol: Mexican Chocolate Ice Cream (Ninja Creami)

**Category:** desserts
**Tags:** #high-protein #batch-prep #low-mess
**Version:** v0.1
**Servings / Yield:** 1 pint (4 servings, ~120g each)
**Prep time:** 5 min
**Freeze time:** 24 hours
**Tools:** Ninja Creami, blender

## Goal / Why this exists
Spiced chocolate ice cream with cinnamon warmth and cayenne heat. For when you want dessert with a kick. The heat builds as you eat — satisfying for spice lovers.

## Nutrition (per serving, ~120g)
- **Calories:** ~115
- **Protein:** ~20g
- **Carbs:** ~9g
- **Fat:** ~2g

## Ingredients
- 240g whole milk
- 60g chocolate protein powder (~2 scoops, casein or blend preferred)
- 15g unsweetened cocoa powder
- 20g granulated sweetener (monk fruit or allulose)
- 3g ground cinnamon
- 1g cayenne pepper (adjust to heat preference; start low)
- 1g instant espresso powder
- Pinch kosher salt

## Method
1. **Combine** all ingredients in a blender. Blend until completely smooth.
2. **Taste** the liquid — cayenne should have noticeable warmth but not burn. Adjust if needed.
3. **Pour** into Ninja Creami pint container to max fill line.
4. **Freeze** for 24 hours minimum. Must be frozen solid.
5. **Process** on ICE CREAM setting. RE-SPIN if needed.
6. **Serve immediately** — the cold dampens heat slightly, then it builds with each bite.

## Success criteria
- Rich chocolate-cinnamon flavor upfront
- Cayenne heat that builds gradually (not immediate burn)
- Smooth, creamy texture

## Variations
- **Extra hot:** Increase cayenne to 2g (for serious heat lovers)
- **Abuelita style:** Reduce cayenne to 0.5g for milder warmth
- **Add crunch:** Mix in 20g crushed cinnamon graham crackers after processing

## Storage & reheat
- Keeps in freezer up to 2 weeks
- Heat perception may intensify slightly after storage; RE-SPIN to serve
